Choosing the Right Wood for 3D Puzzles

When it comes to high-end mechanical puzzles, the choice of timber is not just about aesthetics—it's about engineering. At Artisan Puzzles, we primarily use **American Black Walnut** and **Sugar Maple**.

Why Density Matters

Interlocking puzzles rely on friction and precise tolerances (often within 0.1mm). If a wood is too soft, the joints will wear down over time. If it's too brittle, the intricate tabs might snap during assembly.

"A great puzzle is a conversation between the maker, the material, and the solver."

Walnut provides a beautiful dark contrast and a smooth, oily finish that feels wonderful in the hand. Maple, being denser, is used for the structural 'key' pieces that require the most strength.
