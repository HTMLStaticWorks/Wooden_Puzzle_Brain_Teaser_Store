The Evolution of Laser Cutting in Toy Design

In the past, wooden puzzles were painstakingly carved by hand or cut with scroll saws. While these methods possess a certain charm, they lack the extreme precision required for modern complex mechanical models. Enter the CO2 laser.

Micron-Level Accuracy

Modern laser cutters can achieve a kerf (the width of the cut) as small as 0.1mm. This allows us to design interlocking gears and sliders that fit together perfectly without the need for glue or fasteners.

"Laser technology hasn't replaced the craftsman; it has given the craftsman a sharper, more intelligent tool."

By using computer-aided design (CAD), we can iterate on complex geometries in minutes rather than days. This evolution has democratized high-end puzzle design, allowing boutique workshops to compete with global manufacturers in terms of quality and complexity.
